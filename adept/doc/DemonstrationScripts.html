Saving Documents to a Website

Demo centers around the following procedure: 
  action 'Demo Procedure'(+'$Email message' = "")
   execute:{
    do 'mozilla.Open email'('$Email message');
    do 'mozilla.Save attachments email'('$Email message', "C:\\Documents and Settings\\Default User\\Desktop\\images\\", '$saved attachments 1');
    forall '$file 1' .in. '$saved attachments 1'
     execute:{
      do 'image.OpenFile'('$file 1', '$image 1');
      do 'image.Resize'('$image 1', "medium (800x600 px)", '$resized image 1');
      do 'image.Save'('$resized image 1', "/my/webpage/");
     };
    do 'mozilla.Go to URL'("http://www.mydomain.org/my/webpage/");}
   argtypes:["Desktop.email"]
   properties:{description:"Save the attachments of an email, resize them to 800x600, and then upload them to flickr.com", terse_task_description:"Demo Procedure"}
Demonstration sequence:
  1. Enable Watch Me
  2. Dmeonstrate the above on two-attachment email [See if one attachment will learn a loop] but introduce one or two mistakes
  3. Turn off Watch Me
  4. Learn procedure
  5. [Do we execute this and show failure?]
  6. Edit procedure
  7. Fix error(s)
  8. Execute and show successful completion

Alternative is to replace the image save with an image upload to a site like Flickr. Thisis a more compelling demo as it is more realistic, but would involve either further Firefox instrumentation to handle Javascript, or us building a simple Flickr uploader app (easy to do as Flickr provides a straightforward API, but dubious from a demo standpoint).
